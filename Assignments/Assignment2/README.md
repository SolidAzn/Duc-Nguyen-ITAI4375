## Problem

Before any AI or machine learning model can be built, the underlying data must be understood and validated. This assignment focused on performing a foundational statistical analysis of a real healthcare dataset containing five outpatient clinic visits, analyzing two critical variables: patient age and resting heart rate.

## Approach

I applied descriptive statistical methodology using a transparent four-step process — data extraction, summation, sample verification, and division — to calculate the arithmetic mean for both variables. I then constructed a comprehensive statistical profile including the mean, median, minimum, maximum, and range for each variable. Visual representations (bar charts with mean reference lines) were created to communicate the distribution clearly.

## Results

- **Mean Patient Age:** 45.8 years (range: 28–62 years)
- **Mean Resting Heart Rate:** 76.8 bpm (range: 70–88 bpm, all within the normal 60–100 bpm range)

The close alignment between mean and median (differing by only 0.8 units for both variables) indicated symmetric distributions with no significant skewness, lending confidence to the means as representative values.

## What I Learned

This assignment taught me that the same systematic methodology used for manual statistical calculations — clean extraction, aggregation, validation, and interpretation — directly parallels the data preprocessing pipeline required for AI and machine learning models. A model is only as reliable as the quality of data feeding it. I also came to appreciate that basic statistical literacy is not just a foundational skill but an ongoing necessity, even as AI systems grow more sophisticated.
