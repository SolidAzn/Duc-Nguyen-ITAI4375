# Assignment 3 — Clinical Decision Support Systems: A 2031–2036 Scenario Analysis

**Course:** ITAI 4375 — AI in Healthcare  
**Date:** February 10, 2026  

---

## Problem

Current Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS) suffer from significant limitations: alert fatigue, poor contextual awareness, and an inability to synthesize complex multimodal data. This assignment asked me to envision how AI-powered CDSS will evolve over the next decade, from reactive rule-based alert systems into intelligent clinical copilots.

## Approach

I analyzed the current state of CDSS — including platforms like Glass Health, which uses large language models (LLMs) to generate differential diagnoses from natural language patient descriptions — and identified their key limitations. I then constructed a forward-looking scenario describing four transformative CDSS capabilities expected to emerge between 2031 and 2036, grounded in current research trajectories.

## Results & Key Ideas

The scenario I developed describes future CDSS as systems that are:

1. Embedded directly within EHR workflows rather than existing as standalone tools
2. Capable of processing multimodal data (notes, imaging, genomics, wearables) in real time
3. Proactively surfacing insights rather than waiting for clinician queries
4. Transparent and explainable, reducing the "black box" problem that erodes clinical trust

## What I Learned

This assignment sharpened my ability to think critically and speculatively about AI development. It also clarified a key tension in healthcare AI: the more autonomous and capable a system becomes, the more important human oversight and interpretability become. Technology that clinicians don't trust won't be used — and AI that isn't used helps no one.
