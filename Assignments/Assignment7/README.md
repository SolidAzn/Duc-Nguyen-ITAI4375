# Assignment 7 — AI in Healthcare: A Glossary of Key Terms

**Course:** ITAI 4375 — AI in Healthcare  
**Date:** March 5, 2026  

---

## Problem

As AI adoption in healthcare accelerates — the global healthcare AI market is projected to grow from ~$26.6 billion in 2024 to ~$187.7 billion by 2030 — a persistent vocabulary gap separates the engineers who build AI tools from the clinicians and administrators who use them. This assignment addressed that gap by compiling and explaining 18 core AI terms relevant to healthcare settings.

## Approach

I selected 18 terms that appear frequently in contemporary healthcare AI literature, including machine learning, clinical decision support, natural language processing, deep learning, and predictive analytics. For each term, I provided:

- A plain-language definition accessible to non-technical healthcare professionals
- A concrete clinical or administrative scenario illustrating the term in practice
- Commentary on why the concept matters for patient care and organizational strategy

All definitions were grounded in recent, peer-reviewed publications to ensure accuracy.

## Results & Key Findings

The glossary spans the full stack of healthcare AI — from foundational concepts (supervised vs. unsupervised learning) to applied tools (computer vision in radiology, NLP in EHRs) to governance concepts (model validation, algorithmic bias). Covering this range was intentional: a physician who understands only the clinical side and a data scientist who understands only the technical side are both ill-equipped to evaluate AI tools responsibly.

## What I Learned

Building this glossary reinforced how much of healthcare AI is language — defining terms precisely enough that both a radiologist and a software engineer can have a productive conversation. I also came to appreciate that the best glossary entries don't just define terms but explain *why* the concept exists and what breaks when it's misunderstood.
