# Assignment 1 — Medical Terminology Identification

**Course:** ITAI 4375 — AI in Healthcare  
**Date:** January 16, 2026  

---

## Problem

Healthcare AI systems are only as useful as the people who use and interpret them. Before engaging with AI tools in clinical contexts, it's essential to understand the precise medical language that underpins those environments. This assignment tasked me with identifying and analyzing ten essential medical terms from recent healthcare literature.

## Approach

I selected ten terms spanning several clinical disciplines — oncology, infectious disease, cardiovascular medicine, endocrinology, and patient safety. For each term, I provided:

- A formal definition
- A real-world clinical context example
- An explanation of why the term matters in modern healthcare and informatics

Terms were sourced from recent peer-reviewed journal articles to ensure the definitions reflect current evidence rather than outdated usage.

## Results & What I Learned

This assignment reinforced that precise terminology is the foundation of effective clinical communication. A vocabulary gap between AI developers and clinicians is one of the most underappreciated obstacles to AI adoption in healthcare. When a data scientist and a bedside nurse use the same words to mean different things, patient safety can be compromised.

I also learned how medical terms evolve alongside science — new diagnostic modalities (like liquid biopsies) and therapeutic approaches (like immunotherapy) have generated entirely new vocabulary that all healthcare professionals must understand.

## Key Terms Covered

Immunotherapy, biomarkers, antimicrobial resistance, palliative care, and six additional terms across oncology, cardiovascular medicine, and infectious disease.
